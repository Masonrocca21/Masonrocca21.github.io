  My Master's thesis project: an interactive VR demo built entirely in Unreal Engine 5, where three MetaHuman NPCs are wired together by a real emotional contagion model instead of scripted dialogue trees.
  <br><br>
  The scene, "Il Portale," is a tense checkpoint guarded by three NPCs with distinct social roles: Pasquale (the unshakeable leader), Poldo (the loyal bridge), and Federica (the emotional spark of the group). Space itself is part of the design — NPC positioning defines zones of influence, and how close the player stands determines who can hear, and be moved by, them.
  <br><br>
  There's no dialogue tree here. Each NPC runs on a Blueprint-driven affective engine (six discrete emotions per Ekman's model, propagated through a custom "ripple effect" system) fed by real-time LLM-generated dialogue and rendered live through MetaHuman facial rigs over OpenXR. Winning the scene means realizing you can't out-argue the leader directly — you have to move Federica first and let the emotional wave spread through the group.
  <br><br>
  <img src="img/portfolio/SPECTRA_Blueprint.png" class="img-responsive img-centered" alt="Blueprint architecture in Unreal Engine 5" style="margin: 20px 0; border-radius: 4px;">
  <br>
  I designed and built the full multi-agent architecture in Blueprint: a scalable NPC data structure that decouples each agent's control logic from its physical instance, a centralized middleware for LLM requests, and proxemic/gaze-tracking systems tuned for Meta Quest to keep latency low enough for the illusion of social presence to hold.
  <br><br>
  The system was validated with 21 participants in a VR user study measuring perceived realism, emotional contagion, and spatial awareness — turning this from a tech demo into an empirically tested piece of interactive design.
